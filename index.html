Purpose

This is a Lent rule of life built for discipline, prayer, and physical readiness. It is simple by design: repeatable meals, consistent prayer, and climbing-focused training for a Kalymnos sport climbing trip (late May).

Daily Rule of Life (Chalk)

Morning

  • Wake → no phone for 10 minutes
  • Prayer (Short Trisagion) (2–4 min)
  • Cold finish shower (30–60 sec) (optional)
  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt (30g protein) + fruit
  • Greek: 5 minutes (John verse + 1 travel phrase)
Intention: Begin in attention, not reaction.

Midday

  • Lunch: 1 can lentils + 1 can spinach (heated)
  • Prayer: Jesus Prayer × 30 (or “Lord have mercy” × 50)
  • Walk: 5–10 minutes if possible
Intention: Re-center. Cut the day’s noise.

Evening

  • Training: Climb or TRX (see plan)
  • Protein shake: 30g (mid-afternoon or post-workout)
  • Dinner: beans + rice
  • Optional: granola + milk after climbing day
  • Prayer: Short Trisagion + Psalm 50 (optional)
  • Mobility: 10 minutes
Intention: Close the day quietly. Sleep disciplined.

Ascetic Principles (Moderate)

Food Discipline

  • Same 3 meals daily (simple, humble).
  • No alcohol throughout Lent.
  • No “fasting junk food” (chips/desserts disguised as fasting).
  • No screens while eating (at least once/day).
  • Stop at 80% full.

Attention Discipline

  • No phone for 10 minutes after waking.
  • Midday prayer without negotiation.
  • One quiet walk/week (45–90 min) in silence.
  • One “entertainment fast” evening/week.

Shower Discipline (Simple)

  • Normal shower → finish with 30–60 seconds cold.
  • Use cold finish as “wakeful attention,” not toughness theater.
  • During cold: repeat “Lord have mercy” slowly.

Fail-Safe Rules

  • If prayer is missed → do 2 minutes, not zero.
  • If training is missed → do a 20-minute walk, not zero.
  • If fasting slips → repent and resume next meal.

Training Plan (5.9 → Kalymnos Sport Climbing)

Your Focus (Kalymnos)

  • Steep limestone + tufas = core tension + pump resistance.
  • At 5.9, technique gains give the fastest improvement.
  • Build endurance while protecting fingers & elbows.

Technique Cues for Kalymnos (Read Before You Climb)

10 cues. Keep them simple. Repeat them while climbing. Efficiency beats strength.

  1. Straight arms whenever possible (hang on bones, not biceps).
  2. Hips in on steep routes — bring feet up, don’t pull with arms.
  3. Breathe every move (exhale on hard moves; inhale at rests).
  4. Relax your grip — use only the minimum force needed.
  5. Feet first: find stable feet before committing to hand moves.
  6. Use rests early: shake out before you’re desperate.
  7. Look for the knee/heel: Kalymnos rewards kneebars, heel hooks, drop-knees.
  8. Clip from stable feet (don’t clip “from panic”).
  9. One move at a time: don’t think about the top — execute the next move cleanly.
  10. When pumped: slow down, shake out, breathe, and keep moving.
Rule: If you get pumped, your first correction is not “try harder.” It’s: breathe, relax, find feet, shake out.

Pump Management (5 Rules for Kalymnos)

Kalymnos teaches this lesson fast: pump is a pacing problem as much as a strength problem.

  1. Start easier than you think. First 20 moves should feel “too easy.”
  2. Shake out early. Use rests before you’re desperate. One early shake-out saves the route.
  3. Open hand + minimum grip. Most pump comes from over-gripping holds that don’t require it.
  4. Breath resets the forearms. Exhale on hard moves; deep inhale at rests.
  5. Move between rests calmly. Don’t sprint. Climb in a “steady economy.”
Emergency protocol when pumped: find feet → straight arms → shake one hand → shake the other → breathe → continue.

Climb Session A — Volume + Endurance (Autobelay-friendly)

Goal: aerobic base (anti-pump) + efficient movement.

  • Warm-up 15 min: easy routes + breathing + silent feet
  • Main pick one:
    • ARC: 20–30 min continuous easy climbing (traverse + autobelay laps)
    • Laps: 6–10 laps on a very easy route, rest 1–2 min
    • 4×4: 4 routes at ~70% max, rest 2–3 min, repeat 2 rounds
  • Technique focus: straight arms, hip turns, quiet feet
This session should feel like “work that you can breathe through.”

Climb Session B — Power Endurance (Kalymnos pump training)

Goal: climb while pumped; learn shake-outs and pacing.

  • Warm-up 15 min: easy + a few harder moves
  • Main pick one:
    • Intervals: 6 routes at ~80% max, rest 3 min
    • Back-to-back: 2 routes in a row, rest 4 min, repeat × 3
    • Steep tufa simulation: pick 1 overhung route, climb halfway, downclimb, repeat 3–5 times
  • Skill goal: practice shaking out and breathing under load.
Stop if finger or elbow pain appears. Pump is okay. Pain is not.

Kalymnos Field Guide (What You’ll Need)

What Kalymnos Climbing Feels Like

  • Steep limestone, tufa columns, long pumpy sequences.
  • Endurance + pacing matter more than max strength.
  • Rest positions exist — learn to use them.
  • Climb early or late; mid-day sun can be intense.

Daily Strategy on the Island

  • Warm up on very easy routes. Save skin/tendons.
  • 1 project route max per day (avoid burnout).
  • Stop while you still want more. Come back tomorrow.
  • Drink water before you feel thirsty.

Crag Etiquette (Kalymnos + Greece)

  • Be friendly. Say hello. Offer to share beta respectfully.
  • Don’t hog popular routes — rotate turns.
  • Brush holds gently; don’t damage tufas.
  • Pack out trash. Respect local paths and property.
  • If in doubt: ask kindly in Greek (phrases below).

Greek (Modern + Biblical / Church)

Daily Greek Rule (10 minutes)

  • 5 min Koine / Church: read one John verse aloud 3×
  • 5 min Modern Greek: 3 phrases/day (speak + write once)
Learn like prayer: small, daily, faithful.

Pronunciation Notes (Quick)

  • ευ = “ev/ef” (ευχαριστώ = ef-cha-ri-STO)
  • γ before ε/ι = “y” (γεια = ya)
  • χ = “kh” (Scottish “loch”)
  • Stress mark shows emphasis: καλημέρα (ME)
